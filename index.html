God's Beauty In Nature

Bringing Others Into Praise

God's beauty is ubiquitous because He displays it through His creation. However, sometimes it takes a look through someone else's eyes to help us appreciate it. Once that beauty is recognized, it deserves unrestrained praise for the One who created it.

The Dogwood Tree

Through Him all things were made; without Him nothing was made that has been made. John 1:3

It is true: God is the source of life for all that is, has been, and ever shall be. Proof of that is not only found in scripture, but also in the nature that surrounds us, reflecting His infinite beauty.

But, regardless of His blatant display of beauty that we are constantly immersed in, most of us forget to praise the Creator for His awe-inspiring creation. That is until we are struck by what Dr. Kurt P. Wise calls an "Awe Moment." Dr. Wise defines this "Awe Moment" as a period in which one is so amazed by God's beauty that he or she is rendered speechless. These are, of course, rare, and, on occasion, may spread themselves over years, rather than going by in a moment--as one did for a man living in North Georgia.

"I was going through a rough time in life," he said, recounting the event that had taken place a few years before. Apparently, during this time, he noticed sapling of a dogwood tree in his garden. "It hadn't had a leaf on it for years. It was basically a stick poking up out of the ground."

Being saddened by its dreariness, he began placed his hands on it and prayed for it. Soon, the small dogwood bloomed for the first time in years, and it began to grow. Then, when he moved to a different location, he moved it with him. Ever since, it's grown more and more every year.

Since I heard this story, I've admired the dogwood tree as much as he does. Its branches are now full of leaves, it blooms beautiful flowers every spring, and it grows noticeably larger every year. Every time I look at it, it reminds me to praise God for the beauty he has given us through his creation.

Yet again, events of life and observations of nature have shown me the glory and beauty of God.

For since the creation of the world God's invisible qualities – his eternal power and divine nature – have been clearly seen, being understood from what has been made, so that people are without excuse. Romans 1:20
